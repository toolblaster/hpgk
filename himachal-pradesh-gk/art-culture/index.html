Detailed Culture Notes (विस्तृत संस्कृति नोट्स)

Deep dive into the vibrant traditions of Dev Bhumi. (देवभूमि की जीवंत परंपराओं में गहरा गोता लगाएँ।)

1. Temple Architecture

Himachal is known as the 'Land of Gods' with unique architectural styles adapted to hills.

  • Pent-Roof Style: Oldest style. Lakshana Devi (Bharmour), Shakti Devi (Chatrari).
  • Pagoda Style: Multi-tiered roofs. Hidimba Devi (Manali), Parashar Rishi (Mandi).
  • Nagara Style: Masroor Rock Cut (Kangra - Monolithic), Baijnath (Kangra).
  • Dome Style: Influenced by Mughals/Sikhs. Jwalamukhi, Brajeshwari Devi.
  • Pyramidal Style: Hateshwari Devi (Hatkoti), Shiva Temple (Jubbal).

2. Famous Fairs (प्रसिद्ध मेले)

Fairs are the lifeline of social gathering and trade.

  • Kullu Dussehra: International Fair. Starts on Vijaya Dashami. Lord Raghunath's rath yatra. No Ravan burning.
  • Minjar Fair: Chamba. Maize flower (Minjar) offered to River Ravi/Varuna. Started by Sahil Varman.
  • Shivratri: Mandi. Started by Raja Ajbar Sen (1527). Procession of 200+ deities.
  • Lavi Fair: Rampur. Oldest trade fair. Signed treaty between Bushahr and Tibet.
  • Renuka Fair: Sirmaur. Dedicated to Goddess Renuka (Mother of Parshuram).

3. Festivals (त्यौहार)

Unique hill festivals celebrated with zeal.

  • Losar: New Year festival in Lahaul-Spiti and Kinnaur. Mask dances (Chham).
  • Fulaich: Festival of Flowers in Kinnaur. Villagers collect wild flowers from high peaks.
  • Halda: Lahaul's festival of lights (similar to Diwali). Cedar torches lit.
  • Sair: End of rainy season (September). Offering of crops/walnuts.
  • Nawala: Gaddi tribe festival dedicated to Lord Shiva.

4. Folk Dances (लोक नृत्य)

Rhythmic expressions of joy and devotion.

  • Nati: Most popular dance. Listed in Guinness Book for largest participation.
  • Dangi & Deepak: Performed by Gaddi women in Chamba.
  • Kayang, Bakayang: Kinnaur.
  • Chham: Lahaul-Spiti/Kinnaur monasteries. Monks wear masks to ward off evil.
  • Thoda: War dance of Sirmaur/Shimla. Archery skills displayed.

5. Pahari Painting

Miniature paintings that flourished in hill states.

  • Basohli School: Oldest school. Bold colors. Patron: Raja Kirpal Pal.
  • Kangra School: Most famous. Theme: Radha-Krishna. Golden age under Maharaja Sansar Chand.
  • Chamba Rumal: Pictorial embroidery on handkerchiefs. Patron: Raja Umed Singh (GI Tagged).
  • Arki School: Flourished in Baghal state.

6. Social Customs

Distinct customs of marriage and family.

  • Polyandry: Prevalent in Kinnaur & Lahaul-Spiti (Pandava marriage).
  • Jhanjrara: Marriage by elopement or mutual love.
  • Reet: Custom where a woman could leave her husband for another (Now Abolished).
  • Bata-Sata: Exchange marriage (Barter system of brides).
