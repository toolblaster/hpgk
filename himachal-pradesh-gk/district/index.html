Detailed District Notes (विस्तृत जिला नोट्स)

Key facts and figures for all 12 districts of Himachal Pradesh. (हिमाचल प्रदेश के सभी 12 जिलों के लिए मुख्य तथ्य और आंकड़े।)

1. Bilaspur (बिलासपुर)

Known as 'Kehlur'. Famous for Govind Sagar Lake and Bhakra Dam.

  • Formation: 1st July 1954 (Merged into HP). Earlier a Part-C state.
  • Area: 1,167 sq km (Smallest after Hamirpur). HQ: Bilaspur.
  • History: Founded by Raja Bir Chand (900 AD). Capital shifted from Sunhani to Bilaspur by Dip Chand (1654).
  • Geography: Satluj river divides it. 7 Hill ranges (Sat Dhar) like Naina Devi, Bandla.
  • Fairs: Nalwari Fair (Cattle fair) started by W. Goldstein (1889).
  • Economy: ACC Cement Plant (Barmana), Bhakra Dam.

2. Chamba (चंबा)

The 'Land of Shiva'. Famous for untouched natural beauty and Minjar fair.

  • Formation: 15 April 1948. HQ: Chamba.
  • Area: 6,522 sq km.
  • History: Founded by Raja Maru (550 AD) at Bharmour. Sahil Varman shifted capital to Chamba (920 AD).
  • Geography: Ravi and Chenab rivers. Pangi and Bharmour tribal areas.
  • Culture: Minjar Fair, Sui Fair (Women & Children), Chamba Rumal (GI Tag).
  • Places: Khajjiar ('Mini Switzerland'), Dalhousie, Bharmour.

3. Hamirpur (हमीरपुर)

The 'Literacy Capital' of HP. Smallest district by area.

  • Formation: 1 Sept 1972 (Carved out of Kangra). HQ: Hamirpur.
  • Area: 1,118 sq km. Highest Literacy Rate. Highest road density.
  • History: Part of Katoch dynasty. Town founded by Hamir Chand (1700 AD).
  • Geography: Drained by Beas river (North) and Satluj tributaries (Seer, Kunah, Maan).
  • Places: Deotsidh (Baba Balak Nath Temple), Sujanpur Tira (Holi festival), NIT Hamirpur.

4. Kangra (कांगड़ा)

Most populous district. Land of Trigarta, Tea, and Temples.

  • Formation: 1 Nov 1966 (Merged from Punjab). Tri-furcated in 1972. HQ: Dharamshala.
  • Area: 5,739 sq km. Highest Population in HP.
  • History: Ancient Trigarta. Katoch dynasty. Golden Age under Sansar Chand. Kangra Fort.
  • Geography: Dhauladhar range. Beas river. Pong Dam (Maharana Pratap Sagar) wetland.
  • Places: Dharamshala (Dalai Lama abode), Palampur (Tea City), Baijnath, Masroor.

5. Kinnaur (किन्नौर)

'Land of Gods' (Kinner Desh). Famous for apples and tribal culture.

  • Formation: 1 May 1960 (Carved out of Mahasu). 6th district of HP. HQ: Reckong Peo.
  • Area: 6,401 sq km. Tribal district.
  • Geography: Satluj river bisects it. Peaks: Reo Purgyil (Highest in HP), Kinner Kailash.
  • Culture: Fulaich (Festival of flowers). Lavi Fair (Trade).
  • Places: Kalpa, Sangla Valley (Baspa), Chitkul (Last village), Nako Lake.

6. Kullu (कुल्लू)

'Valley of Gods'. Famous for Dussehra and Tourism.

  • Formation: 1 Nov 1966 (Merged from Punjab). HQ: Kullu.
  • Area: 5,503 sq km.
  • History: Founded by Vihangamani Pal. Idol of Raghunath brought from Ayodhya (1651) by Damodar Das.
  • Geography: Beas river. Parbati, Tirthan, Sainj valleys. Rohtang Pass.
  • Places: Manali, Manikaran (Hot springs), Great Himalayan National Park (UNESCO), Malana.

7. Lahaul & Spiti (लाहौल-स्पीति)

Largest district by area. Cold Desert. Land of Monasteries.

  • Formation: 1 Nov 1966 (Merged from Punjab). HQ: Keylong.
  • Area: 13,835 sq km (Largest - 24.85% of HP). Lowest Population Density.
  • Geography: Chenab (Chandra & Bhaga) and Spiti rivers. Baralacha La, Kunzum La.
  • Culture: Buddhism dominates. Key Monastery (Largest), Tabo (Ajanta of Himalayas).
  • Economy: Seed Potato, Kuth, Peas.

8. Mandi (मंडी)

'Varanasi of Hills' (Chhoti Kashi). Famous for Shivratri Fair.

  • Formation: 15 April 1948 (Merger of Mandi & Suket states). HQ: Mandi.
  • Area: 3,950 sq km.
  • History: Mandi founded by Bahu Sen. Town by Ajbar Sen (1527). Suket by Bir Sen.
  • Geography: Beas river bisects. Balh Valley (Most fertile). Rewalsar Lake.
  • Places: Prashar Lake (Pagoda temple), Janjehli, Jogindernagar (Shanan Power House).

9. Shimla (शिमला)

Capital of HP. Former Summer Capital of British India. 'Queen of Hills'.

  • Formation: 1 Sept 1972 (Mahasu reorganized into Shimla & Solan). HQ: Shimla.
  • Area: 5,131 sq km.
  • History: Discovered by Lt. Ross (1819). Summer Capital (1864). Only district with Urban pop > Rural.
  • Geography: Satluj, Pabbar, Giri rivers. Chanshal Peak. Hatu Peak.
  • Places: Rampur (Lavi Fair), Rohru (Trout), Kufri (Skiing), Hatkoti temple.

10. Sirmaur (सिरमौर)

Southernmost district. Famous for Renuka Lake and Paonta Sahib.

  • Formation: 15 April 1948. HQ: Nahan.
  • Area: 2,825 sq km.
  • History: Founded by Raja Rasloo. Capital shifted to Nahan by Karam Prakash (1621).
  • Geography: Yamuna river (Eastern boundary). Giri river divides it. Churdhar peak.
  • Places: Renuka Lake (Largest natural lake), Paonta Sahib, Suketi Fossil Park.

11. Solan (सोलन)

'Mushroom City of India'. Industrial hub of HP.

  • Formation: 1 Sept 1972 (Carved out of Mahasu). HQ: Solan.
  • Area: 1,936 sq km.
  • History: Named after Shoolini Mata. Baghat state (Solan), Hindur (Nalagarh).
  • Economy: Baddi-Barotiwala-Nalagarh (BBN) industrial belt. Mushroom cultivation. Tomato.
  • Places: Kasauli, Chail (Highest cricket ground), Arki Fort.

12. Una (ऊना)

Gateway to Himachal. Plain areas touching Punjab.

  • Formation: 1 Sept 1972 (Carved out of Kangra). HQ: Una.
  • Area: 1,540 sq km.
  • Geography: Swan River ('River of Sorrows'). Shivalik hills. Jaswan Dun valley.
  • History: Part of Kangra/Jaswan state.
  • Places: Chintpurni (Shakti Peeth), Baba Barbhag Singh Mela (Mairi), Mehatpur.
