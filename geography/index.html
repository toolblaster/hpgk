Detailed Geography Notes (विस्तृत भूगोल नोट्स)

Comprehensive physical geography of Himachal Pradesh.
(हिमाचल प्रदेश का व्यापक भौतिक भूगोल।)

1. Location & Relief (स्थिति और उच्चावच)

Himachal ('Abode of Snow') is situated in the heart of the Western Himalayas.
हिमाचल ('बर्फ का घर') पश्चिमी हिमालय के मध्य में स्थित है।

  • Latitudes: 30°22'40" N to 33°12'40" N.
  • Longitudes: 75°47'55" E to 79°04'20" E.
  • Area: 55,673 sq km (1.7% of India). Larger than Punjab, Haryana, Kerala. 2011 Census Pop: 68,64,602. (क्षेत्रफल 55,673 वर्ग किमी).
  • Boundaries (1170 km): J&K/Ladakh (North), Punjab (West), Haryana (South), Uttarakhand (South-East), Tibet (East - 200km international border). (सीमाएँ: उत्तर में J&K/लद्दाख, पश्चिम में पंजाब, दक्षिण में हरियाणा, पूर्व में तिब्बत).
  • Elevation: Ranges from 350m (Shivalik) to 7000m (Great Himalayas). (ऊंचाई: 350m से 7000m).

2. Mountain Ranges (पर्वत श्रृंखलाएँ)

Three main parallel ranges divide the state physiographically.
तीन मुख्य समानांतर श्रृंखलाएँ राज्य को भौगोलिक रूप से विभाजित करती हैं।

  • Outer Himalayas (Shivalik): Ancient name 'Manak Parbat' (Tresses of Shiva). Alt: 350m-1500m. Comprises Una, Hamirpur, Bilaspur, lower Kangra/Mandi/Solan/Sirmaur. Known for Choes (seasonal streams). (शिवालिक - मानक पर्वत).
  • Lesser Himalayas (Inner): Includes Dhauladhar ('Maulak Parbat' - White Range) mainly in Kangra/Chamba/Mandi and Pir Panjal (Largest range in Lesser Himalayas) separating Kullu from Lahaul. Alt: 1500m-4500m. (धौलाधार - मौलक पर्वत, पीर पंजाल).
  • Great Himalayas (Alpine): Zaskar Range separates Spiti/Kinnaur from Tibet. This zone receives minimal rainfall (Dry Zone). Contains high peaks like Reo Purgyil. Alt: >4500m. (महान हिमालय - जास्कर श्रेणी).

3. Important Peaks (महत्वपूर्ण चोटियाँ)

Highest points in different districts, critical for exams.
विभिन्न जिलों में सबसे ऊंचे बिंदु, परीक्षा के लिए महत्वपूर्ण।

  • Reo Purgyil (6816m): Highest peak in HP (Kinnaur). Part of Zaskar range. (रियो पुरग्यिल - हिमाचल की सबसे ऊंची चोटी).
  • Shilla (6132m): Kinnaur. Often confused as highest, but Reo Purgyil is taller. Named 'Shilla' meaning 'Place of death'. (शिला - किन्नौर).
  • Manirang (6597m): Highest peak on the border of Lahaul-Spiti and Kinnaur. (मनीरंग).
  • Mulkila (6520m): Lahaul-Spiti. 'Mulkila' means 'Silver God'. (मुल्किला - चांदी का देवता).
  • Deo Tibba (6001m) & Indrasan (6220m): Major peaks in Kullu district. (देव टिब्बा और इंद्रसाण - कुल्लू).
  • Hanuman Tibba (5860m): Highest in Dhauladhar range (Kangra/Kullu border). (हनुमान टिब्बा).
  • Churdhar (3647m): Highest peak in Sirmaur (Shivaliks). Known as 'Churchandni' (Moonlight peak). (चूड़धार - सिरमौर).

4. Mountain Passes (पर्वतीय दर्रे)

Strategic gateways connecting different valleys and districts.
विभिन्न घाटियों और जिलों को जोड़ने वाले रणनीतिक रास्ते।

  • Rohtang Pass (3978m): 'Pile of Corpses' (Bhrigu Tungh). Connects Kullu & Lahaul. Atal Tunnel (9.02 km) is built here. First Englishman to reach: William Moorcroft (1820). (रोहतांग दर्रा - लाशों का ढेर).
  • Baralacha La (4890m): 'Pass with cross roads on summit'. Connects Lahaul & Ladakh (Zanskar). Source of Chenab river (Suraj Tal). (बारालाचा ला - लाहौल और लद्दाख को जोड़ता है).
  • Shipki La (4500m): Kinnaur to Tibet. Satluj river enters India here. Old Silk Route branch. (शिपकी ला - सतलुज का प्रवेश द्वार).
  • Kunzum La (4551m): Connects Lahaul & Spiti. Gateway to Spiti Valley. (कुंजुम ला - स्पीति का प्रवेश द्वार).
  • Sach Pass (4414m): Connects Chamba & Pangi valley. Known for difficult terrain and late opening. (साच दर्रा - चंबा और पांगी).
  • Indrahar Pass (4320m): Connects Kangra (Dharamshala) and Chamba (Bharmour). (इन्द्रहार दर्रा).

5. Glaciers (ग्लेशियर / हिमनद)

Locally known as 'Shigri'. Major source of HP's river systems.
स्थानीय भाषा में 'शिगड़ी' कहा जाता है। हिमाचल की नदी प्रणालियों का मुख्य स्रोत।

  • Bara Shigri: Largest glacier in HP (Lahaul). ~25 km long, 3 km wide. Feeds Chandra river. First surveyed by Walker and Pascoe (1851). (बड़ा शिगड़ी - लाहौल, सबसे बड़ा).
  • Sonapani: Lahaul valley. Visible from Rohtang. Surveyed by Walker (1906). (सोनापानी - लाहौल).
  • Gyephang: Lahaul. Its peak resembles Matterhorn of Switzerland. Named after the presiding deity of Lahaul (Gyephang Lha). (गेफांग - स्विट्जरलैंड के मैटरहर्न जैसा).
  • Lady of Keylong: Visible from Keylong. Named by Lady Elsighday (British). Looks like a woman carrying a load on her back. (लेडी ऑफ केलांग).
  • Bhadal & Tantgari: Located in Bara Banghal (Kangra). Feed Ravi river. (भादल और तंतगरी - रावी के स्रोत).
  • Beas Kund: Near Rohtang. Source of Beas river. Sage Vyas meditated here. (व्यास कुंड).

6. Famous Valleys (प्रसिद्ध घाटियाँ)

Fertile lands, fruit bowls, and scenic beauty of the state.
उपजाऊ भूमि, फलों के कटोरे और राज्य की प्राकृतिक सुंदरता।

  • Kangra Valley: 'Veer Bhumi'. Dun-type valley between Dhauladhar and Shivalik. Famous for Tea cultivation and Sobha Singh Art Gallery (Andretta). (कांगड़ा घाटी - वीर भूमि, चाय की खेती).
  • Kullu Valley: 'Dev Ghati' (Valley of Gods). Formed by Beas river. Famous for Apple orchards, Dussehra, and Shawls. Ends at Rohtang. (कुल्लू घाटी - देव घाटी, सेब).
  • Sangla (Baspa) Valley: Most beautiful valley in Kinnaur. Famous for Bhojpatra trees, Ogla/Fafra, and Chitkul (Last village). (सांगला/बास्पा घाटी - किन्नौर).
  • Pin Valley: Spiti. Cold desert national park. Home to Snow Leopard and Ibex. (पिन घाटी - स्पीति, हिम तेंदुआ).
  • Paonta (Kiarda Dun) Valley: Sirmaur. Formed by Yamuna and Giri rivers. Highly fertile. Sikh pilgrimage site. (पांवटा/कियारा दून घाटी - सिरमौर).
  • Balh Valley: Mandi (Sundernagar). Most fertile valley of HP. Indo-German agricultural project started here (1962). (बल्ह घाटी - मंडी, सबसे उपजाऊ).
  • Pattan Valley: Lahaul. Where Chandra and Bhaga rivers flow (Chenab). Known for Potato and Hops cultivation. (पत्तन घाटी - लाहौल).

7. Climate Zones (जलवायु क्षेत्र)

Variation from hot sub-tropical to freezing alpine due to elevation.
ऊंचाई के कारण गर्म उप-उष्णकटिबंधीय से लेकर जमने वाले अल्पाइन तक विविधता।

  • Sub-Tropical (Continental Monsoon): Low hills (Una, Bilaspur, Hamirpur, Paonta, Nalagarh). Hot summers (up to 40°C), moderate winters. Citrus fruits grow well. (उप-उष्णकटिबंधीय - ऊना, बिलासपुर).
  • Sub-Temperate (Wet): Mid hills (Shimla, Kullu, Solan, Dharamshala, Palampur). Mild summers, snowy winters. Highest rainfall in Dharamshala (3400mm). (उप-शीतोष्ण - शिमला, धर्मशाला).
  • Alpine/Arctic (Dry): High hills (Kinnaur, Lahaul-Spiti, Pangi). Dry and very cold. Semi-arctic conditions. 'Rain Shadow Area'. Spiti is the 'Dryest place' (50mm rain). (अल्पाइन/शुष्क - किन्नौर, स्पीति).
  • Seasons: Taundi (Summer), Hyund (Winter), Barsat (Monsoon). Sharad (Autumn) is short. (स्थानीय नाम - तौंदी, ह्युंद, बरसात).

8. Soils of HP (हिमाचल की मिट्टी)

Types of soil based on altitude and vegetation cover.
ऊंचाई और वनस्पति आवरण के आधार पर मिट्टी के प्रकार।

  • Low Hill Soil: Sandy loam to loam. Nitrogen and Phosphorus deficient. Good for Wheat, Maize, Citrus fruits, Mango, Ginger. (निचली पहाड़ी मिट्टी - रेतीली दोमट).
  • Mid Hill Soil: Loam to Clay loam. Greyish color. Good for Stone fruits (Peach, Plum, Apricot), Potatoes, Off-season vegetables. (मध्य पहाड़ी मिट्टी - दोमट से चिकनी).
  • High Hill Soil: Forest soil, acidic in nature. Rich in humus but deep. Excellent for Apples, Pears, Walnuts, Conifers. (उच्च पहाड़ी मिट्टी - वन मिट्टी, अम्लीय).
  • Cold Desert Soil: Sandy, low organic matter, dry. Lahaul-Spiti and Kinnaur. Potatoes, Peas, Kuth, Hops grown here with irrigation. (शीत मरुस्थल मिट्टी - रेतीली, शुष्क).

9. Natural Hazards (प्राकृतिक खतरे)

Geographical challenges faced by the state due to fragile ecology.
नाजुक पारिस्थितिकी के कारण राज्य द्वारा सामना की जाने वाली भौगोलिक चुनौतियाँ।

  • Earthquakes: HP falls in Seismic Zone IV and V (Highest risk). 1905 Kangra Earthquake (7.8 mag) killed 20,000+ people. 1975 Kinnaur Earthquake was also major. (भूकंप - 1905 कांगड़ा भूकंप).
  • Landslides: Frequent in monsoon along NH-5 (Kinnaur, Maling Nala) and NH-3 (Kullu). Caused by deforestation, road construction, and hydro projects. (भूस्खलन).
  • Cloud Bursts: Sudden heavy rain (>100mm/hr) in short duration. Common in Kullu, Kinnaur, Shimla. E.g., Samej cloudburst (2024), Chirgaon (1997). (बादल फटना).
  • Avalanches: Massive snow slides in Lahaul-Spiti and Kinnaur winters blocking access and damaging property. (हिमस्खलन).
  • Glacial Lake Outburst Floods (GLOF): Risk from Parechu lake (Tibet) threatening Spiti/Satluj basin. (ग्लेशियर झील बाढ़).
