Detailed Geography Notes (विस्तृत भूगोल नोट्स)

Comprehensive physical geography of Himachal Pradesh.
(हिमाचल प्रदेश का व्यापक भौतिक भूगोल।)

1. Location & Relief (स्थिति और उच्चावच)

Himachal ('Abode of Snow') is situated in the heart of the Western Himalayas.
हिमाचल ('बर्फ का घर') पश्चिमी हिमालय के मध्य में स्थित है।

  • Latitudes: 30°22'40" N to 33°12'40" N.
  • Longitudes: 75°47'55" E to 79°04'20" E.
  • Area: 55,673 sq km (1.7% of India). Area-wise Rank: 18th (Before J&K split, was 17th). Larger than Punjab, Haryana, Kerala.
  • Boundaries (1170 km): J&K (North), Ladakh (North-East), Punjab (West), Haryana (South), Uttarakhand (South-East), UP (Touching Sirmaur), Tibet (East - 200km border).
  • Elevation: Increases from West to East and South to North. Ranges from 350m (Shivalik foot-hills) to 7025m (Shilla peak).

2. Mountain Ranges (पर्वत श्रृंखलाएँ)

Three main parallel ranges divide the state physiographically.
तीन मुख्य समानांतर श्रृंखलाएँ राज्य को भौगोलिक रूप से विभाजित करती हैं।

  • Outer Himalayas (Shivalik): 'Manak Parbat' (Tresses of Shiva). Alt: 350m-1500m. Width: 10-50 km. Comprises Una, Hamirpur, Bilaspur, Paonta Valley, Nahan. Known for Choes (seasonal streams) and loose conglomerates.
  • Lesser Himalayas (Inner): Dhauladhar ('Maulak Parbat' - White Range) mainly in Kangra/Chamba/Mandi. Pir Panjal is the largest range in Lesser Himalayas (separates Kullu from Lahaul, Sutlej cuts it at Rampur). Alt: 1500m-4500m.
  • Great Himalayas (Alpine): Zaskar Range separates Spiti/Kinnaur from Tibet. This zone receives minimal rainfall (Dry Zone). Contains highest peaks like Reo Purgyil. Alt: >4500m.

3. Important Peaks (महत्वपूर्ण चोटियाँ)

Highest points in different districts, critical for exams.
विभिन्न जिलों में सबसे ऊंचे बिंदु, परीक्षा के लिए महत्वपूर्ण।

  • Kinnaur: Reo Purgyil (6816m - Highest in HP), Shilla (6132m - 'Place of Death'), Kinner Kailash (6500m), Shipki (6608m).
  • Lahaul-Spiti: Manirang (6597m), Mulkila (6520m - 'Silver God'), Gyephang (5870m - Matterhorn of HP), Shikari Beh (6200m).
  • Kullu: Deo Tibba (6001m), Indrasan (6220m), Dibibokri Pyramid (6400m), Solang (5975m).
  • Kangra/Chamba: Hanuman Tibba (5860m - Highest in Dhauladhar), Pir Panjal (5972m), Manimahesh Kailash (5653m).
  • Shimla: Chanshal Peak (4520m - Source of Pabbar), Hatu Peak (3400m), Shali Tibba.
  • Sirmaur: Churdhar (3647m - Highest in Shivaliks/Outer Himalayas). Known as 'Churchandni'.

4. Mountain Passes (पर्वतीय दर्रे)

Strategic gateways connecting different valleys and districts.
विभिन्न घाटियों और जिलों को जोड़ने वाले रणनीतिक रास्ते।

  • Rohtang Pass (3978m): 'Pile of Corpses' (Bhrigu Tungh). Connects Kullu & Lahaul. Atal Tunnel (9.02 km) built under it.
  • Baralacha La (4890m): 'Pass with cross roads on summit' (Zanskar, Ladakh, Lahaul, Spiti). Source of Chenab (Suraj Tal).
  • Shipki La (4500m): Kinnaur to Tibet. Satluj enters India here. Old Hindustan-Tibet Road.
  • Kunzum La (4551m): Connects Lahaul & Spiti. Gateway to Spiti Valley. Goddess Kunzum Mata temple on top.
  • Sach Pass (4414m): Connects Chamba & Pangi valley. Shortest route to Killar.
  • Pin Parvati Pass (5319m): Connects Kullu (Parvati Valley) and Spiti (Pin Valley). Difficult trek.
  • Indrahar Pass (4320m): Dhauladhar range. Connects Kangra (Dharamshala) and Chamba (Bharmour).

5. Glaciers (ग्लेशियर / हिमनद)

Locally known as 'Shigri'. Major source of HP's river systems.
स्थानीय भाषा में 'शिगड़ी' कहा जाता है। हिमाचल की नदी प्रणालियों का मुख्य स्रोत।

  • Bara Shigri: Largest glacier in HP (Lahaul). ~25 km long, 3 km wide. Feeds Chandra river. First surveyed by Walker and Pascoe (1851).
  • Sonapani: Lahaul valley. Visible from Rohtang. Surveyed by Walker (1906).
  • Gyephang: Lahaul. Its peak resembles Matterhorn of Switzerland. Named after the presiding deity of Lahaul (Gyephang Lha).
  • Lady of Keylong: Visible from Keylong. Named by Lady Elsighday (British). Looks like a woman carrying a load on her back. Snow melts only in peak summer.
  • Bhadal & Tantgari: Located in Bara Banghal (Kangra). Feed Ravi river.
  • Beas Kund: Near Rohtang. Source of Beas river. Sage Vyas meditated here.
  • Miyar Glacier: Lahaul. ~24km long. Feeds Miyar Nala (Tributary of Chenab).

6. Famous Valleys (प्रसिद्ध घाटियाँ)

Fertile lands, fruit bowls, and scenic beauty of the state.
उपजाऊ भूमि, फलों के कटोरे और राज्य की प्राकृतिक सुंदरता।

  • Kangra Valley: 'Veer Bhumi'. Dun-type valley between Dhauladhar and Shivalik. Famous for Tea cultivation (since 1849) and Sobha Singh Art Gallery (Andretta).
  • Kullu Valley: 'Dev Ghati' (Valley of Gods). Formed by Beas river. Famous for Apple orchards (First planted by Capt. Lee at Bandrol), Dussehra, and Shawls. Ends at Rohtang.
  • Sangla (Baspa) Valley: Most beautiful valley in Kinnaur. Famous for Bhojpatra trees, Ogla/Fafra, and Chitkul (Last village on Indo-Tibetan border).
  • Pin Valley: Spiti. Cold desert national park. Home to Snow Leopard and Ibex. Known for 'Chham' dance.
  • Paonta (Kiarda Dun) Valley: Sirmaur. Formed by Yamuna and Giri rivers. Highly fertile. Sikh pilgrimage site (Paonta Sahib). Separated from Dehradun by Yamuna.
  • Balh Valley: Mandi (Sundernagar). Most fertile valley of HP. Indo-German agricultural project started here (1962). Known as 'Mini Punjab'.
  • Pattan Valley: Lahaul. Where Chandra and Bhaga rivers flow (Chenab). Known for Potato and Hops cultivation. Distinct culture mixture of Hindu-Buddhist.

7. Climate Zones (जलवायु क्षेत्र)

Variation from hot sub-tropical to freezing alpine due to elevation.
ऊंचाई के कारण गर्म उप-उष्णकटिबंधीय से लेकर जमने वाले अल्पाइन तक विविधता।

  • Sub-Tropical (Continental Monsoon): Low hills (Una, Bilaspur, Hamirpur, Paonta, Nalagarh). Hot summers (up to 40°C), moderate winters. Citrus fruits grow well.
  • Sub-Temperate (Wet): Mid hills (Shimla, Kullu, Solan, Dharamshala, Palampur). Mild summers, snowy winters. Highest rainfall in Dharamshala (3400mm). Ideal for stone fruits.
  • Alpine/Arctic (Dry): High hills (Kinnaur, Lahaul-Spiti, Pangi). Dry and very cold. Semi-arctic conditions. 'Rain Shadow Area'. Spiti is the 'Dryest place' (50mm rain). No monsoon effect.
  • Seasons: Taundi (Summer), Hyund (Winter), Barsat (Monsoon). Sharad (Autumn) is short.

8. Soils of HP (हिमाचल की मिट्टी)

Types of soil based on altitude and vegetation cover.
ऊंचाई और वनस्पति आवरण के आधार पर मिट्टी के प्रकार।

  • Low Hill Soil: Sandy loam to loam. Nitrogen and Phosphorus deficient. Good for Wheat, Maize, Citrus fruits, Mango, Ginger (Sirmaur is largest producer).
  • Mid Hill Soil: Loam to Clay loam. Greyish color. Good for Stone fruits (Peach, Plum, Apricot), Potatoes, Off-season vegetables.
  • High Hill Soil: Forest soil, acidic in nature. Rich in humus but deep. Excellent for Apples (Royal Delicious), Pears, Walnuts, Conifers.
  • Cold Desert Soil: Sandy, low organic matter, dry. Lahaul-Spiti and Kinnaur. Potatoes (Lahaul Seed Potato), Peas, Kuth, Hops grown here with irrigation.

9. Natural Hazards (प्राकृतिक खतरे)

Geographical challenges faced by the state due to fragile ecology.
नाजुक पारिस्थितिकी के कारण राज्य द्वारा सामना की जाने वाली भौगोलिक चुनौतियाँ।

  • Earthquakes: HP falls in Seismic Zone IV and V (Highest risk). 1905 Kangra Earthquake (7.8 mag) killed 20,000+ people. 1975 Kinnaur Earthquake was also major.
  • Landslides: Frequent in monsoon along NH-5 (Kinnaur, Maling Nala) and NH-3 (Kullu). Caused by deforestation, road construction, and hydro projects.
  • Cloud Bursts: Sudden heavy rain (>100mm/hr) in short duration. Common in Kullu, Kinnaur, Shimla. E.g., Samej cloudburst (2024), Chirgaon (1997).
  • Avalanches: Massive snow slides in Lahaul-Spiti and Kinnaur winters blocking access and damaging property. Rohtang tunnel provides relief.
  • Glacial Lake Outburst Floods (GLOF): Risk from Parechu lake (Tibet) threatening Spiti/Satluj basin. Artificial lakes formed by landslides (e.g. Satluj blockage 2000).
