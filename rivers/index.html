Detailed Study Notes

Comprehensive analysis of the drainage system for competitive exams.

1. The Satluj (Vedic: Sutudri)

Originating from Rakas Tal near Mansarovar in Tibet, the Satluj enters HP at Shipki La (Kinnaur). It cuts through the Great Himalaya and the Zaskar ranges. It flows through Kinnaur, Shimla, Kullu, Solan, Mandi, and Bilaspur before entering Punjab at Bhakra.

  • Length in HP: 320 km (Longest river in HP).
  • Catchment Area: 20,000 sq km.
  • Tributaries: Spiti, Ropa, Taiti, Kashang, Mulgaon, Yula, Wanger, Throng, Ropa. Spiti river joins Satluj at Namgia (Khab).
  • Major Projects: Bhakra Nangal (1325 MW), Nathpa Jhakri (1500 MW), Karcham Wangtoo (1000 MW), Rampur (412 MW).
  • Towns: Kalpa, Rampur, Tattapani, Bilaspur.

2. The Beas (Vedic: Arjikiya)

Originates from Beas Kund near Rohtang Pass in the Pir Panjal range. It flows south through Kullu valley, then turns west, flowing through Mandi, Hamirpur, and Kangra. It leaves HP at Mirthal to enter Punjab.

  • Sanskrit Name: Vipasha.
  • Length in HP: 256 km.
  • Catchment Area: 12,000 sq km.
  • Tributaries: Parbati (joins at Shamshi), Spin, Hurla, Sainj, Uhl (joins at Mandi), Banganga, Chakki, Gaj, Dehr.
  • Mythology: It is believed that Alexander the Great stopped his invasion at this river (Hyphasis).

3. The Ravi (Vedic: Parushni)

Originates from Bara Banghal (Kangra) as a joint stream formed by Bhadal and Tant Gari glaciers. It flows westward through the Chamba valley (Ravi Valley) between Dhauladhar and Pir Panjal ranges.

  • Sanskrit Name: Iravati.
  • Length in HP: 158 km.
  • Catchment Area: 5,451 sq km.
  • Tributaries: Bhadal, Tant Gari, Siul, Baira, Chirchind Nala.
  • Towns: Chamba town is situated on its right bank. It leaves HP at Kheri.

4. The Chenab (Vedic: Asikni)

Formed by the confluence of Chandra and Bhaga rivers at Tandi (Lahaul). It is the largest river in HP by volume of water. It flows through Lahaul and the Pangi valley of Chamba.

  • Sanskrit Name: Chandrabhaga.
  • Length in HP: 122 km.
  • Catchment Area: 7,500 sq km.
  • Route: It enters Pangi valley at Bhujind and leaves HP at Sansari Nala to enter the Paddar valley of Kashmir.
  • Speciality: Known for Gold content in ancient times.

5. The Yamuna (Vedic: Kalindi)

It originates from Yamunotri in Garhwal hills. It enters HP at Khodar Majri in Sirmaur and leaves at Tajewala. It forms the eastern boundary with Uttarakhand.

  • Length in HP: Shortest course in HP.
  • Catchment Area: 2,320 sq km.
  • Tributaries: Giri (Giri Ganga), Tons (largest tributary), Pabbar, Bata, Andhra.
  • Relation: Associated with the Sun God (Surya).
