Detailed Study Notes (विस्तृत अध्ययन नोट्स)

Comprehensive analysis of the drainage system for competitive exams. (प्रतियोगी परीक्षाओं के लिए जल निकासी प्रणाली का व्यापक विश्लेषण।)

1. The Satluj (सतलुज - लाल नदी)

The Satluj is the longest river in Himachal Pradesh, cutting across the formidable Great Himalaya and Zaskar ranges. It is an antecedent river.
सतलुज हिमाचल प्रदेश की सबसे लंबी नदी है, जो महान हिमालय और जास्कर पर्वतमाला को काटती है। यह एक पूर्ववर्ती नदी है।

  • Vedic Name: Sutudri | Sanskrit Name: Shatadru (सुतुद्री / शतद्रु).
  • Origin: Rakas Tal near Mansarovar (Tibet). Known as 'Langqen Zangbo' (Elephant River). (उद्गम: राक्षस ताल, तिब्बत).
  • Entry into HP: Enters at Shipki La (Pass) in Kinnaur at 6,608 meters. (प्रवेश: शिपकी ला, किन्नौर).
  • Exit from HP: Leaves HP at Bhakra village (Naila) to enter Punjab (Nangal). (निकास: भाखड़ा गाँव).
  • Total Length in HP: 320 km (कुल लंबाई).
  • Catchment Area: 20,000 sq km (Largest in HP) (जलग्रहण क्षेत्र).
  • Course: Kinnaur, Shimla, Kullu, Solan, Mandi, and Bilaspur districts.

Major Tributaries (प्रमुख सहायक नदियाँ):

  • Spiti River (स्पीति): Joins Satluj at Namgia (Khab). Tributaries: Tegpo, Kabzian. (खाब संगम में मिलती है).
  • Baspa River (बास्पा): Joins Satluj at Karcham (Kalpa). (करछम में मिलती है).
  • Nogli Khad (नोगली खड्ड): Joins below Rampur Bushahr.
  • Soan River (स्वान): 'River of Sorrows' in Una district. (ऊना की 'दुखों की नदी').
  • Others: Bispah, Ropa, Taiti, Kashang, Mulgaon, Yula, Wanger, Throng, Ali Khad, Seer Khad, Gamber Khad.

Prominent Towns (प्रमुख शहर): Kalpa, Rampur, Tattapani, Bilaspur, Namgia.

Hydro Projects (जलविद्युत परियोजनाएं): Bhakra Nangal (1325 MW), Nathpa Jhakri (1500 MW), Karcham Wangtoo (1000 MW), Rampur (412 MW), Kol Dam (800 MW), Sanjay Vidyut (Bhaba).

2. The Beas (व्यास)

Historically significant, marking the eastern border of Alexander's conquest (326 BC). Flows entirely in India.
ऐतिहासिक रूप से महत्वपूर्ण, सिकंदर की विजय (326 ईसा पूर्व) की पूर्वी सीमा। यह पूरी तरह से भारत में बहती है।

  • Vedic Name: Arjikiya | Sanskrit Name: Vipasha (अर्जीकिया / विपाशा).
  • Greek Name: Hyphasis (हाइपासिस).
  • Origin: Beas Kund near Rohtang Pass, Pir Panjal Range. (उद्गम: व्यास कुंड, रोहतांग दर्रा).
  • Exit from HP: Leaves at Mirthal (Kathgarh) to enter Punjab. (निकास: मिर्थल).
  • Total Length in HP: 256 km.
  • Catchment Area: 12,000 sq km.
  • Course: Kullu, Mandi, Hamirpur, and Kangra. Forms deep gorge at Larji.

Major Tributaries (प्रमुख सहायक नदियाँ):

  • Parbati River (पार्वती): Largest tributary. Rises from Mantalai Lake. Joins at Shamshi. (सबसे बड़ी सहायक नदी, शमशी में मिलती है).
  • Banganga (बाणगंगा): Joins in Kangra valley.
  • Chakki (चक्की): Joins near Pathankot.
  • Sainj & Tirthan (सैंज और तीर्थन): Join at Larji.
  • Uhl (उहल): Rises from Thamsar Pass, joins near Mandi.
  • Others: Luni, Binwa, Harla, Solang, Manalsu, Sujoin, Fozal, Sarvari, Kunah, Maan, Gaj, Dehar, Awa, Neugal.

Prominent Towns (प्रमुख शहर): Manali, Kullu, Mandi, Pandoh, Sujanpur, Nadaun, Dehra-Gopipur.

Hydro Projects: Larji (126 MW), Baner, Neugal, Beas-Satluj Link (Pandoh Dam), Pong Dam (Maharana Pratap Sagar).

3. The Ravi (रावी)

The life-line of the Chamba valley, flowing between Dhauladhar and Pir Panjal. Known for its rugged course.
चंबा घाटी की जीवन रेखा, धौलाधार और पीर पंजाल के बीच बहती है।

  • Vedic Name: Parushni | Sanskrit Name: Iravati (परुष्णी / इरावती).
  • Greek Name: Hydraotes (हाइड्राओट्स).
  • Origin: Bara Banghal (Kangra), formed by Bhadal and Tant Gari glaciers. (उद्गम: बड़ा भंगाल).
  • Exit from HP: Leaves Chamba at Kheri to enter J&K (near Madhopur). (निकास: खेड़ी).
  • Total Length in HP: 158 km.
  • Catchment Area: 5,451 sq km.
  • Course: Primarily flows through Chamba district.

Major Tributaries (प्रमुख सहायक नदियाँ):

  • Bhadal & Tant Gari (भादल और तंत गारी): Source streams.
  • Siul (सियूल): Major tributary from the north.
  • Baira (बैरा): Feeds into Siul.
  • Budhil (बुधिल): Rises from Kugti Pass, joins at Kharamukh.
  • Others: Tundah, Chirchind Nala, Chatrari.

Prominent Towns (प्रमुख शहर): Chamba (Right Bank), Bharmour.

Hydro Projects: Chamera I (540 MW), Chamera II (300 MW), Chamera III (231 MW), Baira Siul (180 MW), Thein Dam (Ranjit Sagar Dam).

4. The Chenab (चिनाब / चंद्रभागा)

The largest river in HP by volume of water. It flows through the remote and rugged valleys of Lahaul and Pangi. Locally known as "Chandrabhaga".
जल आयतन की दृष्टि से हिमाचल की सबसे बड़ी नदी। यह लाहौल और पांगी की दुर्गम घाटियों से होकर बहती है। स्थानीय रूप से "चंद्रभागा" के रूप में जानी जाती है।

  • Vedic Name: Asikni (असिकनी).
  • Greek Name: Acesines (अकेसिन्स).
  • Origin: Formed by the confluence of Chandra and Bhaga rivers at Tandi (Lahaul). Legend says Draupadi breathed her last here. (उद्गम: तांडी में चंद्रा और भागा का संगम).
  • Chandra River (चंद्रा): Originates from Chandra Tal glacier. Flows through a desolate region (Rangoli).
  • Bhaga River (भागा): Originates from Suraj Tal (near Baralacha Pass). Meets Chandra at Tandi.
  • Exit from HP: Leaves Pangi valley at Sansari Nala to enter Paddar valley (Kashmir). (निकास: संसारी नाला).
  • Total Length in HP: 122 km.
  • Catchment Area: 7,500 sq km.
  • Course: Enters Pangi valley of Chamba at Bhujind. It flows through Tandi, Udaipur, and Killar.

Major Tributaries (प्रमुख सहायक नदियाँ):

  • Miyar Nala (मियार नाला): A major tributary joining at Udaipur in Lahaul. Originates from Miyar glacier.
  • Saicher Nala (सैचर नाला): Joins near Pangi (Sach Khas).
  • Thirot Nala: Joins near Thirot.
  • Others: Sohal, Bhut Nala, Marau, Shansha Nala, Lujai Nala.

Special Note: Gold is found in the sands of Chenab (hence called Gold River). (रेत में सोना पाया जाता है).

Hydro Projects: Seli, Raoli (500 MW), Miyar, Thirot (4.5 MW), Dugar (500 MW), Sach Khas.

5. The Yamuna (यमुना)

The shortest river course in HP, yet vital as it forms the eastern boundary with Uttarakhand. It is a major tributary of the Ganga and holds immense mythological significance.
हिमाचल में सबसे छोटी नदी, उत्तराखंड के साथ पूर्वी सीमा बनाती है। यह गंगा की एक प्रमुख सहायक नदी है।

  • Vedic Name: Kalindi (कालिंदी).
  • Origin: Yamunotri (Garhwal Hills, Uttarakhand). Associated with Kalind Parvat. (उद्गम: यमुनोत्री).
  • Entry into HP: Khodar Majri (Sirmaur). (प्रवेश: खोदर माजरी).
  • Exit from HP: Tajewala (enters Haryana). (निकास: ताजेवाला).
  • Total Length in HP: 22 km.
  • Catchment Area: 2,320 sq km.
  • Relation: Mythologically related to the Sun God (Surya). (सूर्य देव से संबंधित).

Major Tributaries (प्रमुख सहायक नदियाँ):

  • Tons River (टोंस): Largest tributary. Rises from Rupin and Supin streams (joining at Naitwar). Joins Yamuna at Kalsi. Legend connects it to the tears of Mandodari (Ravana's wife).
  • Giri River (गिरी): Rises from Kupar peak (Jubbal). Divides Sirmaur into Cis-Giri and Trans-Giri. Joins Yamuna at Paonta Sahib (Mokkampur).
  • Pabbar River (पब्बर): Rises from Chander Nahan Lake (Rohru) near Chanshal Peak. Joins Tons near Tiuni. Famous for Trout fish.
  • Bata River (बाटा): Originates from Dharthi range (Silla Ki Dhar). Joins Yamuna at Bata Mandi. Known for the battle of Bhangani (Guru Gobind Singh).
  • Andhra River: Tributary of Pabbar, rises near Chirgaon.
  • Patsari: Tributary of Pabbar, rises near Khara-Pathar.
  • Jalal: Tributary of Giri, rises from Dharthi ranges. joins Giri at Dadahu.
  • Markanda: Rises near Baraban (Kiarda Dun valley), flows towards Haryana.

Prominent Towns: Paonta Sahib (Famous Gurudwara).

Hydro Projects: Giri Bata Project, Yamuna Hydel Project (shared), Renuka Dam (Proposed - National Project), Kishau Dam (Proposed on Tons), Sawra Kuddu (111 MW on Pabbar).

6. Other Important Streams (अन्य महत्वपूर्ण खड्ड)

Apart from the five major river systems, Himachal Pradesh has numerous smaller streams (Khads) that are crucial for local irrigation and ecology.

  • Swan River (स्वान नदी): Flows through the Una district. Historically known as the "River of Sorrows" of HP due to seasonal floods and soil erosion. Extensive channelization projects have now tamed it. It divides Una district into two almost equal parts. Its source is near Daulatpur. (ऊना की 'दुखों की नदी', दौलतपुर के पास उद्गम).
  • Sirsa River (सिरसा नदी): Flows through the Nalagarh valley in Solan district. The industrial hub of Baddi-Barotiwala-Nalagarh lies in its catchment. It joins the Satluj river near Ropar in Punjab. (नालागढ़ घाटी से बहती है, रोपड़ के पास सतलुज में मिलती है).
  • Ghamber Khad (गंभर खड्ड): A significant tributary of the Satluj. It rises from the Tara Devi hills near Shimla and joins the Satluj in Bilaspur district. It is famous for the bridge on the Shimla-Bilaspur road. (तारा देवी हिल्स से निकलती है).
  • Imla and Bimla (इमला और बिमला): Two famous streams flowing through the fertile Karsog valley of Mandi district. They are tributaries of the Satluj river and are vital for the agriculture of the valley. (करसोग घाटी, मंडी).
  • Chakki Khad (चक्की खड्ड): Rises from the Dhauladhar range and defines the boundary between Himachal Pradesh and Punjab near Pathankot. It joins the Beas river. (हिमाचल और पंजाब की सीमा बनाती है).
  • Markanda River (मारकंडा नदी): Flows in Sirmaur district through the Kiarda Dun valley. The famous Suketi Fossil Park is located on its bank. It flows into Haryana. (कियारा दून घाटी, सिरमौर).
  • Bata River (बाटा नदी): Originates from the Dharthi range in Sirmaur (Silla Ki Dhar). It flows through the Paonta valley and joins the Yamuna at Bata Mandi. Legend says its course was changed by a curse. (धारथी श्रेणी से उद्गम, यमुना की सहायक).
  • Kunah and Maan Khads (कुनाह और मान खड्ड): Two major seasonal streams in Hamirpur district that drain into the Beas river. Maan Khad joins Beas near Nadaun. (हमीरपुर जिले की प्रमुख धाराएं).
  • Seer Khad (सीर खड्ड): The largest tributary of Satluj in Bilaspur district. It rises near Sarkaghat in Mandi. (बिलासपुर में सतलुज की सबसे बड़ी सहायक नदी).
  • Ali Khad (अली खड्ड): Rises near Arki (Solan) and enters Bilaspur to join Satluj at Beri Ghat. (अर्की से निकलती है).

7. Drainage Patterns (अपवाह तंत्र)

  • Antecedent Drainage (पूर्ववर्ती अपवाह): Satluj existed before Himalayas rose, cutting deep gorges. (सतलुज इसका उदाहरण है).
  • Dendritic Pattern (वृक्षाकार प्रतिरूप): Beas and Ravi basins resemble tree branches. (व्यास और रावी बेसिन).
  • Parallel Drainage (समानांतर अपवाह): Streams in Shivalik hills flow parallel.

8. Lakes & Water Bodies (जुड़ी हुई झीलें)

  • Govind Sagar (Bilaspur): Man-made reservoir on Satluj (Bhakra Dam). (गोविंद सागर - सतलुज).
  • Pong Dam Lake (Kangra): On Beas. Ramsar wetland. (पोंग बांध - व्यास).
  • Chamera Lake (Chamba): On Ravi. (चमेरा - रावी).
  • Renuka Lake (Sirmaur): Fed by Giri basin. Largest natural lake. (रेणुका - गिरी).
  • Chandra Tal & Suraj Tal (Lahaul): Sources of Chenab. (चंद्रा और सूरज ताल - चिनाब).

9. Environmental Concerns (पर्यावरण संबंधी चिंताएँ)

  • Siltation (गाद): High silt in Satluj affects hydro projects like Nathpa Jhakri.
  • Glacial Retreat (ग्लेशियर का पिघलना): Melting of glaciers like Bara Shigri threatens long-term flow.
  • Pollution (प्रदूषण): Industrial waste in Sirsa and Markanda rivers.
  • Flash Floods (बाढ़): Steep gradients of Beas/Satluj cause sudden floods.
