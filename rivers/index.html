River System of Himachal Pradesh

Introduction

Himachal Pradesh provides water to both the Indus and Ganges basins. The drainage system of the region is composed of five major rivers: Chenab, Ravi, Beas, Satluj, and Yamuna.

1. The Satluj (Vedic: Sutudri)

Originating from Rakas Tal near Mansarovar in Tibet, the Satluj enters HP at Shipki La (Kinnaur). It flows through Kinnaur, Shimla, Kullu, Solan, Mandi, and Bilaspur before entering Punjab at Bhakra.

  • Length in HP: 320 km (Longest river in HP).
  • Tributaries: Spiti, Ropa, Taiti, Kashang, Mulgaon, Yula, Wanger, Throng.
  • Major Projects: Bhakra Nangal, Nathpa Jhakri, Karcham Wangtoo.

2. The Beas (Vedic: Arjikiya)

Originates from Beas Kund near Rohtang Pass. It flows through Kullu, Mandi, Hamirpur, and Kangra. It leaves HP at Mirthal to enter Punjab.

  • Length in HP: 256 km.
  • Tributaries: Parbati, Spin, Hurla, Sainj, Uhl, Banganga, Chakki.
  • Mythology: It is believed that Alexander the Great stopped his invasion at this river.

3. The Ravi (Vedic: Parushni)

Originates from Bara Banghal (Kangra). It flows westward through the Chamba valley between Dhauladhar and Pir Panjal ranges.

  • Length in HP: 158 km.
  • Tributaries: Bhadal, Tant Gari, Siul, Baira.
  • Towns: Chamba town is situated on its right bank.

4. The Chenab (Vedic: Asikni)

Formed by the confluence of Chandra and Bhaga rivers at Tandi (Lahaul). It is the largest river in HP by volume of water.

  • Length in HP: 122 km.
  • Route: Flows through Lahaul and Pangi valley of Chamba before entering J&K at Sansari Nala.
