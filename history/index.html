History of Himachal Pradesh
हिमाचल प्रदेश का इतिहास

Why Study History? (इतिहास क्यों पढ़ें?)

History forms the backbone of the HPAS syllabus. Understanding the lineage of ancient Hill States, their wars with Gurkhas, and the administrative evolution under British rule is crucial for both Prelims and Mains.

इतिहास HPAS पाठ्यक्रम की रीढ़ है। प्राचीन पहाड़ी राज्यों की वंशावली, गोरखाओं के साथ उनके युद्ध और ब्रिटिश शासन के तहत प्रशासनिक विकास को समझना महत्वपूर्ण है।

Timeline Covered (समयरेखा)

  • Ancient (प्राचीन): Trigarta, Kuluta, Audumbaras.
  • Medieval (मध्यकालीन): Mughals vs Hill Rajas, Sansar Chand.
  • Modern (आधुनिक): Gorkha Wars (1805-1815), 1857 Revolt, Prajamandal Movement, Statehood (1971).

Historical Overview (ऐतिहासिक अवलोकन)

From the Mahabharata era to the formation of the 18th state of India.

1. Ancient Janapadas (प्राचीन जनपद)

Himachal was inhabited by tribes like Dasas, Khasas, and Kinnars. Major ancient states included:

  • Trigarta (Kangra): Founded by Susharma Chandra (ally of Kauravas). Capital: Nagarkot (Multan earlier). Oldest princely state. (सबसे पुरानी रियासत, संस्थापक: सुशर्मा चंद्र).
  • Kuluta (Kullu): Founded by Behangamani Pal. Mentioned in Ramayana and Vishnu Purana. Old capital: Jagatsukh. (विहंगमणि पाल द्वारा स्थापित, प्राचीन राजधानी: जगतसुख).
  • Audumbaras: Tribal republic in Pathankot/Kangra region. Worshipped Shiva (Vishwamitra connection). Famous for copper coins. (पठानकोट/कांगड़ा क्षेत्र, शिव उपासक, तांबे और चांदी के सिक्के).
  • Kunindas: Lived between Ambala and Saharanpur (Sirmaur/Shimla hills). Coins found in Tappa Mewa. Ruler: Amoghabhuti. (सिरमौर/शिमला पहाड़ियों के बीच).

2. Medieval Period (मध्यकालीन काल)

The hill states faced invasions from Ghaznavids, Tughlaqs, and later came under Mughal suzerainty.

  • Mahmud Ghazni (1009 AD): Looted Nagarkot fort and Brajeshwari temple during the 4th expedition. (नगरकोट किले और ब्रजेश्वरी मंदिर को लूटा).
  • Firoz Shah Tughlaq (1365 AD): Attacked Kangra (Raja Rup Chand). Took 1300 Sanskrit books from Jwalamukhi to translate into Persian (Dalail-i-Firozshahi). (1300 संस्कृत पुस्तकें फारसी अनुवाद के लिए ले गए).
  • Timur (1398 AD): Invaded Shivalik hills. Hindur (Nalagarh) Raja Alam Chand helped him. (शिवालिक पहाड़ियों पर आक्रमण, हिंदूर राजा ने मदद की).
  • Mughals: Akbar besieged Kangra (1572). Jahangir captured Kangra Fort (1620) - first Mughal Emperor to visit. (जहांगीर ने कांगड़ा किले पर कब्जा किया, मस्जिद बनवाई).

3. The Gorkha Invasion (गोरखा आक्रमण)

The expanding Gorkha empire under Amar Singh Thapa clashed with Sansar Chand and later the British.

  • Battle of Mahal Morian (1806): Gorkhas defeated Sansar Chand of Kangra. Sansar Chand took refuge in Kangra Fort. (गोरखाओं ने संसार चंद को हराया).
  • Invitation: Raja Mahan Chand of Bilaspur invited Gorkhas to fight Sansar Chand. (बिलासपुर के राजा महान चंद ने आमंत्रण दिया).
  • Anglo-Gorkha War (1814-15): British (Ochterlony) vs Gorkhas (Amar Singh Thapa). Key Battles: Jaitak, Malaon. (अंग्रेज बनाम गोरखा).
  • Treaty of Sugauli (1815): Ended the war. Gorkhas ceded HP territories to British. Satluj became the boundary. (युद्ध समाप्त, सतलुज सीमा बनी).

4. British Rule & 1857 Revolt (ब्रिटिश शासन)

After 1815, British paramountcy was established. The 1857 revolt saw mixed reactions from Hill Chiefs.

  • 1857 Revolt (विद्रोह): Raja Shamsher Singh of Bushahr was hostile to British. Raja of Kahlur (Bilaspur) helped British. (बुशहर के राजा ने विरोध किया).
  • Subathu: First Nasiri Battalion (Gurkhas) formed here. Oldest Cantonment established in 1815. (पहली नासिरी बटालियन).
  • Kasauli: Soldiers revolted but were suppressed. (कसौली विद्रोह).
  • Loyalty: Most hill rajas (Chamba, Mandi, Bilaspur) remained loyal to British to save their states. (अधिकतर राजा वफादार रहे).

5. Prajamandal Movement (प्रजामंडल आंदोलन)

People's movement against the feudal rule of Rajas and British interference.

  • Dhami Firing (1939): "Black Day" in HP history. Bhagmal Sautha led the protest demanding end to Begar. Police fired on peaceful crowd. (धामी गोलीकांड - काला दिवस).
  • Pajhota Andolan (1942): Extension of Quit India Movement in Sirmaur. Leaders: Vaid Surat Singh, Mian Chu-Chu. (पझोता आंदोलन, सिरमौर).
  • Suket Satyagraha (1948): Led by Pt. Padam Dev against Raja of Suket. Led to merger of Suket into India. (सुकेत सत्याग्रह, पं. पदम देव).
  • Bhai Do Na Pai Do: Movement in Kangra/Hills echoing "No Revenue, No Soldiers". (भाई दो न पाई दो आंदोलन).

6. Formation of State (राज्य का गठन)

  • 15 April 1948: HP formed as Chief Commissioner's Province (30 states merged). 4 Districts: Chamba, Mandi, Mahasu, Sirmour. (मुख्य आयुक्त प्रांत, 4 जिले).
  • 1951: Became 'Part C' State under Indian Constitution. First Lt. Governor: Major Gen Himmat Singh. ('सी' श्रेणी राज्य).
  • 1 Nov 1966: Punjab Reorganization. Kangra, Kullu, Shimla, Lahaul-Spiti merged into HP. Area doubled. (विशाल हिमाचल का निर्माण).
  • 25 Jan 1971: Full Statehood (18th State). Inaugurated by Indira Gandhi at Ridge, Shimla. (पूर्ण राज्यत्व, 18वां राज्य).

7. Famous Freedom Fighters (प्रसिद्ध स्वतंत्रता सेनानी)

  • Dr. Y.S. Parmar: Known as the 'Founder of Himachal Pradesh'. First Chief Minister. (हिमाचल निर्माता, प्रथम मुख्यमंत्री).
  • Baba Kanshi Ram: 'Pahari Gandhi' (Title by Nehru) & 'Paharan Da Bulbul'. (पहाड़ी गांधी, काले कपड़े पहनने की शपथ).
  • Pt. Padam Dev: 'Kaviraj'. Led the Suket Satyagraha. (कविराज, सुकेत सत्याग्रह).
  • Vaid Surat Singh: Key leader of the Pajhota Movement in Sirmaur. (पझोता आंदोलन के नेता).

8. Key Historical Treaties (प्रमुख ऐतिहासिक संधियाँ)

  • Treaty of Jwalamukhi (1809): Between Sansar Chand & Maharaja Ranjit Singh. Kangra Fort ceded to Sikhs. (ज्वालामुखी संधि).
  • Treaty of Sugauli (1815): Between British & Gurkhas. Defined British control over HP. (सुगौली संधि).
  • Treaty of Amritsar (1846): Between British & Gulab Singh. Created J&K state. (अमृतसर संधि).
  • Treaty of Malaon (1815): Surrender of Amar Singh Thapa to Ochterlony. (मलौण संधि).

9. Historical Forts (ऐतिहासिक किले)

  • Kangra Fort: Built by Susharma Chandra. Oldest fort in HP. (कांगड़ा किला - सबसे पुराना).
  • Kamlah Fort: Located in Mandi. Famous for its invincibility until 1840. (कमलाह किला, मंडी).
  • Jaitak Fort: Built by Ranjor Singh Thapa (Gurkha) in Sirmaur. (जैटक किला, सिरमौर).
  • Naggar Castle: Ancient seat of Kullu Rajas. Built by Raja Sidh Singh. (नग्गर कैसल, कुल्लू).
  • Arki Fort: Capital of Baghal state. Mural paintings in Arki style are famous. (अर्की किला).
