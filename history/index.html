History of Himachal Pradesh
हिमाचल प्रदेश का इतिहास

Why Study History? (इतिहास क्यों पढ़ें?)

History forms the backbone of the HPAS syllabus. Understanding the lineage of ancient Hill States, their wars with Gurkhas, and the administrative evolution under British rule is crucial for both Prelims and Mains.

इतिहास HPAS पाठ्यक्रम की रीढ़ है। प्राचीन पहाड़ी राज्यों की वंशावली, गोरखाओं के साथ उनके युद्ध और ब्रिटिश शासन के तहत प्रशासनिक विकास को समझना महत्वपूर्ण है।

Timeline Covered (समयरेखा)

  • Ancient (प्राचीन): Trigarta, Kuluta, Audumbaras.
  • Medieval (मध्यकालीन): Mughals vs Hill Rajas, Sansar Chand.
  • Modern (आधुनिक): Gorkha Wars (1805-1815), 1857 Revolt, Prajamandal Movement, Statehood (1971).

Historical Overview (ऐतिहासिक अवलोकन)

From the Mahabharata era to the formation of the 18th state of India.

1. Ancient Janapadas (प्राचीन जनपद)

Himachal was inhabited by tribes like Dasas, Khasas, and Kinnars. Major ancient states included:

  • Trigarta (Kangra): Founded by Susharma Chandra (ally of Kauravas). Capital: Nagarkot (Multan earlier). Oldest princely state in HP. Mentioned by Panini as 'Ayudhjivi Sangha'. (सबसे पुरानी रियासत, संस्थापक: सुशर्मा चंद्र, पाणिनि द्वारा उल्लेखित).
  • Kuluta (Kullu): Founded by Behangamani Pal. Mentioned in Ramayana and Vishnu Purana. Old capital: Jagatsukh. Famous for ancient democracy (Malana). (विहंगमणि पाल द्वारा स्थापित, प्राचीन राजधानी: जगतसुख).
  • Audumbaras: Tribal republic in Pathankot/Kangra/Nurpur region. Worshipped Shiva (Vishwamitra connection). Famous for copper and silver coins found in Kangra and Hamirpur. (पठानकोट/कांगड़ा क्षेत्र, शिव उपासक, तांबे और चांदी के सिक्के).
  • Kunindas: Lived between Ambala and Saharanpur (Sirmaur/Shimla hills). Coins found in Tappa Mewa and Jwalamukhi. Ruler: Amoghabhuti (Most famous king). (सिरमौर/शिमला पहाड़ियों के बीच, राजा अमोधभूति).

2. Medieval Period (मध्यकालीन काल)

The hill states faced invasions from Ghaznavids, Tughlaqs, and later came under Mughal suzerainty.

  • Mahmud Ghazni (1009 AD): Looted Nagarkot fort and Brajeshwari temple during the 4th expedition. Carried away immense wealth. (नगरकोट किले और ब्रजेश्वरी मंदिर को लूटा).
  • Firoz Shah Tughlaq (1365 AD): Attacked Kangra (Raja Rup Chand). Took 1300 Sanskrit books from Jwalamukhi library to translate into Persian (Dalail-i-Firozshahi) by Izz-ud-din Khalid Khani. (1300 संस्कृत पुस्तकें फारसी अनुवाद के लिए ले गए).
  • Timur (1398 AD): Invaded Shivalik hills on his way back from Delhi. Hindur (Nalagarh) Raja Alam Chand helped him and saved his state. (शिवालिक पहाड़ियों पर आक्रमण, हिंदूर राजा ने मदद की).
  • Mughals: Akbar besieged Kangra (1572). Jahangir captured Kangra Fort (1620) - first Mughal Emperor to visit. Built a mosque inside the fort. (जहांगीर ने कांगड़ा किले पर कब्जा किया, मस्जिद बनवाई).

3. The Gorkha Invasion (गोरखा आक्रमण)

The expanding Gorkha empire under Amar Singh Thapa clashed with Sansar Chand and later the British.

  • Battle of Mahal Morian (1806): Gorkhas defeated Sansar Chand of Kangra at Mahal Morian (Hamirpur). Sansar Chand took refuge in Kangra Fort for 4 years. (गोरखाओं ने संसार चंद को हराया).
  • Invitation: Raja Mahan Chand of Bilaspur invited Gorkhas to fight Sansar Chand due to his expansionist policy. (बिलासपुर के राजा महान चंद ने आमंत्रण दिया).
  • Anglo-Gorkha War (1814-15): British (David Ochterlony) vs Gorkhas (Amar Singh Thapa). Key Battles: Jaitak (Sirmaur), Malaon (Solan). Bhakti Thapa showed immense bravery. (अंग्रेज बनाम गोरखा).
  • Treaty of Sugauli (1815): Ended the war. Signed at Sugauli. Gorkhas ceded HP territories (Shimla, Kinnaur, Sirmaur) to British. Satluj became the boundary. (युद्ध समाप्त, सतलुज सीमा बनी).

4. British Rule & 1857 Revolt (ब्रिटिश शासन)

After 1815, British paramountcy was established. The 1857 revolt saw mixed reactions from Hill Chiefs.

  • 1857 Revolt (विद्रोह): Raja Shamsher Singh of Bushahr was hostile to British and refused tribute. Raja of Kahlur (Bilaspur) & Mandi helped British. Ram Singh Pathania revolted in Nurpur. (बुशहर के राजा ने विरोध किया, नूरपुर में वजीर राम सिंह).
  • Subathu: First Nasiri Battalion (Gurkhas) formed here. Oldest Cantonment established in 1815. (पहली नासिरी बटालियन, सबसे पुराना छावनी).
  • Kasauli: Soldiers of the Nasiri battalion revolted and looted the treasury but were suppressed. (कसौली विद्रोह).
  • Loyalty: Most hill rajas (Chamba, Mandi, Bilaspur, Sirmaur) remained loyal to British to save their states and received Sanads. (अधिकतर राजा वफादार रहे).

5. Prajamandal Movement (प्रजामंडल आंदोलन)

People's movement against the feudal rule of Rajas and British interference, demanding democratic rights.

  • Dhami Firing (1939): "Black Day" in HP history. Bhagmal Sautha led the protest demanding end to Begar. Police fired on peaceful crowd, 2 died. (धामी गोलीकांड - काला दिवस).
  • Pajhota Andolan (1942): Extension of Quit India Movement in Sirmaur. Leaders: Vaid Surat Singh, Mian Chu-Chu. Slogan "Bhai Do Na Pai" started here. (पझोता आंदोलन, सिरमौर).
  • Suket Satyagraha (1948): Led by Pt. Padam Dev against Raja of Suket. Led to merger of Suket into India, accelerating HP formation. (सुकेत सत्याग्रह, पं. पदम देव).
  • Bhai Do Na Pai Do: Movement in Kangra/Hills echoing "No Revenue, No Soldiers" to British. (भाई दो न पाई दो आंदोलन).

6. Formation of State (राज्य का गठन)

  • 15 April 1948: HP formed as Chief Commissioner's Province (30 princely states merged). 4 Districts: Chamba, Mandi, Mahasu, Sirmour. First Chief Commissioner: N.C. Mehta. (मुख्य आयुक्त प्रांत, 4 जिले).
  • 1951: Became 'Part C' State under Indian Constitution. First Lt. Governor: Major Gen Himmat Singh. ('सी' श्रेणी राज्य).
  • 1 Nov 1966: Punjab Reorganization. Kangra, Kullu, Shimla, Lahaul-Spiti merged into HP. Area doubled to 55,673 sq km. (विशाल हिमाचल का निर्माण).
  • 25 Jan 1971: Full Statehood (18th State). Inaugurated by Indira Gandhi at Ridge, Shimla amid snowfall. First CM: Dr. Y.S. Parmar. (पूर्ण राज्यत्व, 18वां राज्य).

6A. C-class State (1948-1956) ('सी' श्रेणी राज्य)

After being a Chief Commissioner's Province from 1948 to 1951, Himachal Pradesh became a Part-C state.
1948 से 1951 तक मुख्य आयुक्त प्रांत रहने के बाद, हिमाचल प्रदेश एक 'सी' श्रेणी राज्य बन गया।

  • 1951 Act: The Government of Part C States Act, 1951 was passed. (भाग सी राज्य अधिनियम पारित).
  • First Elections (पहले चुनाव): Held in 1952. Congress won 24 out of 36 seats. (1952 में चुनाव, कांग्रेस ने 24 सीटें जीतीं).
  • First CM (प्रथम मुख्यमंत्री): Dr. Y.S. Parmar took oath on 24th March 1952. (डॉ. वाई.एस. परमार ने शपथ ली).
  • Lt. Governor (उपराज्यपाल): Replaced the Chief Commissioner. Major General Himmat Singh was the first (1st March 1952). (मुख्य आयुक्त की जगह उपराज्यपाल).

6B. Union Territory (1956-1971) (केंद्र शासित प्रदेश)

Following the States Reorganization Commission (Fazl Ali Commission) report, HP became a Union Territory.
राज्य पुनर्गठन आयोग (फजल अली आयोग) की रिपोर्ट के बाद, हिमाचल प्रदेश एक केंद्र शासित प्रदेश बन गया।

  • 1 Nov 1956: Status changed from Part-C State to Union Territory. (भाग-सी राज्य से केंद्र शासित प्रदेश बना).
  • Territorial Council (क्षेत्रीय परिषद): The Legislative Assembly was abolished and replaced by a Territorial Council. Thakur Karam Singh was elected Chairman. (विधानसभा समाप्त, क्षेत्रीय परिषद गठित).
  • Restoration (बहाली): The Legislative Assembly was restored on 1st July 1963. Dr. Y.S. Parmar became CM again. (विधानसभा बहाल).
  • 1966 Merger (विलय): Massive expansion with merger of Punjab hill areas, but remained a UT. (पंजाब पहाड़ी क्षेत्रों का विलय).

6C. Full Statehood (1971) (पूर्ण राज्यत्व)

The final step in the political journey of Himachal Pradesh.
हिमाचल प्रदेश की राजनीतिक यात्रा का अंतिम चरण।

  • 31 July 1970: The State of Himachal Pradesh Act was passed by the Parliament. (संसद ने हिमाचल प्रदेश राज्य अधिनियम पारित किया).
  • 25 Jan 1971: Prime Minister Indira Gandhi inaugurated the new state at Ridge, Shimla. (प्रधानमंत्री इंदिरा गांधी ने नए राज्य का उद्घाटन किया).
  • Status (स्थिति): Became the 18th State of the Indian Union. (भारतीय संघ का 18वां राज्य बना).
  • Significance (महत्व): First hill state to achieve this status after J&K, proving that hill economies could be viable. (जम्मू-कश्मीर के बाद यह दर्जा पाने वाला पहला पहाड़ी राज्य).

7. Famous Freedom Fighters (प्रसिद्ध स्वतंत्रता सेनानी)

  • Dr. Y.S. Parmar: Known as the 'Founder of Himachal Pradesh'. First Chief Minister. Led the Himalayan Hill States Regional Council. (हिमाचल निर्माता, प्रथम मुख्यमंत्री).
  • Baba Kanshi Ram: 'Pahari Gandhi' (Title by Nehru) & 'Paharan Da Bulbul' (Title by Sarojini Naidu). Vowed to wear black clothes until freedom after execution of Bhagat Singh. (पहाड़ी गांधी, काले कपड़े पहनने की शपथ).
  • Pt. Padam Dev: 'Kaviraj'. Led the Suket Satyagraha. Founding member of HP Congress. (कविराज, सुकेत सत्याग्रह).
  • Vaid Surat Singh: Key leader of the Pajhota Movement in Sirmaur. Advocated for non-violent resistance. (पझोता आंदोलन के नेता).

8. Key Historical Treaties (प्रमुख ऐतिहासिक संधियाँ)

  • Treaty of Jwalamukhi (1809): Between Sansar Chand & Maharaja Ranjit Singh. Kangra Fort ceded to Sikhs. (ज्वालामुखी संधि).
  • Treaty of Sugauli (1815): Between British & Gurkhas. Defined British control over HP hills west of Kali river. (सुगौली संधि).
  • Treaty of Amritsar (1846): Between British & Gulab Singh. Created J&K state, transferred Chamba and Lahaul to Gulab Singh (later Chamba restored). (अमृतसर संधि).
  • Treaty of Malaon (1815): Surrender of Amar Singh Thapa to Ochterlony, ending Gurkha rule in HP. (मलौण संधि).

9. Historical Forts (ऐतिहासिक किले)

  • Kangra Fort: Built by Susharma Chandra. Oldest fort in HP. Captured by Mahmud Ghazni, Firoz Tughlaq, Jahangir, Ranjit Singh, and British. (कांगड़ा किला - सबसे पुराना).
  • Kamlah Fort: Located in Mandi. Built by Raja Suraj Sen. Famous for its invincibility until 1840 when Sikhs captured it. (कमलाह किला, मंडी).
  • Jaitak Fort: Built by Ranjor Singh Thapa (Gurkha) in Sirmaur using material from Nahan fort. Site of major battle in 1814. (जैटक किला, सिरमौर).
  • Naggar Castle: Ancient seat of Kullu Rajas. Built by Raja Sidh Singh. Example of Kath-Kuni architecture. (नग्गर कैसल, कुल्लू).
  • Arki Fort: Capital of Baghal state. Mural paintings in Arki style are famous. (अर्की किला).
