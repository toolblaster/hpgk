History of Himachal Pradesh
हिमाचल प्रदेश का इतिहास

Why Study History? (इतिहास क्यों पढ़ें?)

History forms the backbone of the HPAS syllabus. Understanding the lineage of ancient Hill States, their wars with Gurkhas, and the administrative evolution under British rule is crucial for both Prelims and Mains.

इतिहास HPAS पाठ्यक्रम की रीढ़ है। प्राचीन पहाड़ी राज्यों की वंशावली, गोरखाओं के साथ उनके युद्ध और ब्रिटिश शासन के तहत प्रशासनिक विकास को समझना महत्वपूर्ण है।

Timeline Covered (समयरेखा)

  • Ancient (प्राचीन): Trigarta, Kuluta, Audumbaras.
  • Medieval (मध्यकालीन): Mughals vs Hill Rajas, Sansar Chand.
  • Modern (आधुनिक): Gorkha Wars (1805-1815), 1857 Revolt, Prajamandal Movement, Statehood (1971).

Pro Tip (प्रो टिप)

Focus on "Who built what" (forts/temples) and "Who fought whom" (battles). Attempt the quiz to memorize these facts.

Historical Overview (ऐतिहासिक अवलोकन)

From the Mahabharata era to the formation of the 18th state of India.

1. Ancient Janapadas (प्राचीन जनपद)

Himachal was inhabited by tribes like Dasas, Khasas, and Kinnars. Major ancient states included:

  • Trigarta (Kangra): Founded by Susharma Chandra (ally of Kauravas). Capital: Nagarkot (Multan earlier). Oldest princely state. (सबसे पुरानी रियासत).
  • Kuluta (Kullu): Founded by Behangamani Pal. Mentioned in Ramayana and Vishnu Purana. Old capital: Jagatsukh. (विहंगमणि पाल द्वारा स्थापित).
  • Audumbaras: Tribal republic in Pathankot/Kangra region. Worshipped Shiva (Vishwamitra connection). Famous for coins. (सिक्कों के लिए प्रसिद्ध).
  • Kunindas: Lived between Ambala and Saharanpur (Sirmaur/Shimla hills). Coins found in Tappa Mewa.

2. Medieval Period (मध्यकालीन काल)

The hill states faced invasions from Ghaznavids, Tughlaqs, and later came under Mughal suzerainty.

  • Mahmud Ghazni (1009 AD): Looted Nagarkot fort and Brajeshwari temple.
  • Firoz Shah Tughlaq (1365 AD): Attacked Kangra (Raja Rup Chand). Took 1300 Sanskrit books from Jwalamukhi to translate into Persian (Dalail-i-Firozshahi).
  • Timur (1398 AD): Invaded Shivalik hills. Hindur (Nalagarh) Raja Alam Chand helped him.
  • Mughals: Akbar besieged Kangra (1572). Jahangir captured Kangra Fort (1620) - first Mughal Emperor to visit.

3. The Gorkha Invasion (गोरखा आक्रमण)

The expanding Gorkha empire under Amar Singh Thapa clashed with Sansar Chand and later the British.

  • Battle of Mahal Morian (1806): Gorkhas defeated Sansar Chand of Kangra. Sansar Chand took refuge in Kangra Fort.
  • Invitation: Raja Mahan Chand of Bilaspur invited Gorkhas to fight Sansar Chand.
  • Anglo-Gorkha War (1814-15): British (Ochterlony) vs Gorkhas (Amar Singh Thapa). Battles at Jaitak, Malaon.
  • Treaty of Sugauli (1815): Ended the war. Gorkhas ceded HP territories to British. Satluj became the boundary.

4. British Rule & 1857 Revolt (ब्रिटिश शासन)

After 1815, British paramountcy was established. The 1857 revolt saw mixed reactions.

  • 1857 Revolt: Raja Shamsher Singh of Bushahr was hostile to British. Raja of Kahlur (Bilaspur) helped British.
  • Subathu: First Nasiri Battalion formed here.
  • Kasauli: Soldiers revolted but were suppressed.
  • Loyalty: Most hill rajas (Chamba, Mandi, Bilaspur) remained loyal to British to save their states.

5. Prajamandal Movement (प्रजामंडल आंदोलन)

People's movement against the feudal rule of Rajas and British interference.

  • Dhami Firing (1939): "Black Day" in HP history. Bhagmal Sautha led the protest. Police fired on peaceful crowd.
  • Pajhota Andolan (1942): Extension of Quit India Movement in Sirmaur. Leaders: Vaid Surat Singh, Mian Chu-Chu.
  • Suket Satyagraha (1948): Led by Pt. Padam Dev against Raja of Suket. Led to merger of Suket.
  • Bhai Do Na Pai Do: Movement in Kangra/Hills echoing "No Revenue, No Soldiers".

6. Formation of State (राज्य का गठन)

  • 15 April 1948: HP formed as Chief Commissioner's Province (30 states merged). 4 Districts: Chamba, Mandi, Mahasu, Sirmour.
  • 1951: Became 'Part C' State.
  • 1 Nov 1966: Punjab Reorganization. Kangra, Kullu, Shimla, Lahaul-Spiti merged into HP. Area doubled.
  • 25 Jan 1971: Full Statehood (18th State). Inaugurated by Indira Gandhi at Ridge, Shimla.
